Insights on the news you receive and issues you care about.

About the Project

In today's America, constructive debate about social issues has broken down. We don’t just disagree on policies anymore. We disagree about what’s true, and what news can be trusted.

Middle|Ground aims to shine a light on the imbalanced and sometimes inaccurate news we receive through social media which inform our opinions, to promote greater perspective and dialogue about issues of social importance.

Inspired by a powerful "Blue Feed Red Feed" visual created by the Wall Street Journal in 2016, Middle|Ground v1 offers side-by-side comparison of recent social media streams from the news sources deemed most trusted by people with “liberal” and “conservative” ideologies. Users can search by topic of interest and see how these topics are represented on social media by the news sources deemed "most reliable" in a large recent study by the Pew Research Center, a respected nonpartisan think tank and research hub.

In addition, Middle|Ground harnesses the Google Fact Check API to show any claims related to the topic which have been fact-checked, along with any conclusion reached on whether the claims are partly or mostly true or false.
